Research Experience



    Throughout my Undergraduate education, I have remained deeply involved in a number of research projects. My primary body of works has been under Prof. Saskia Mordijck on constructing a Machine-Learning pipeline to infer experimental transport conditions in tokamak reactors. This project has allowed me to wear many hats, diving into Computational Modeling, Numerical Methods, High-Performance Computing, Neural Networks, Reinforcement Learning, Uncertainty Quantification, Bayesian Optimization, MCMC, and more.


    I have been responsible for nearly all aspects of the project and afforded a high level of independence regarding its direction. I regularly present aspects of this project and its progress to my research group as well as the MIT Neutrals Group under Prof. Jerry Hughes. In addition, I have presented it at APS DPP 2024 and 2025, winning the outstanding poster award in 2024. I have attached some of the presentation materials for this project below.
